Here are key learnings from building and understanding Supply Chain Builder:

📦 Supply chain is more than delivery — it connects suppliers, production, storage, transport, and customers into one business system.

⚖️ Every decision has trade-offs — reducing cost can increase risk, while improving speed may increase expenses.

🏭 Factory location impacts business performance through labor cost, lead time, and customer reach.

🚚 Transportation choice affects speed, sustainability, and customer satisfaction differently.

📦 Inventory management is strategic — too little stock risks shortages, too much increases storage costs.

🌍 Supplier diversification reduces dependency risk and improves business resilience.

📊 Supply chain decisions should be measured using KPIs like cost, delivery speed, risk, and customer satisfaction.

🔄 Business success comes from balancing efficiency and customer experience, not optimizing only one metric.

🧠 Simulation-based learning helps connect theory with real business scenarios.

💼 A data-driven mindset is essential for modern supply chain and business decision-making.


This topic also highlights how analytical thinking and scenario testing can support real-world business strategy.
