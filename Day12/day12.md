Here are the key learnings from my resume analysis that i should internalize for my job search:

 **My Biggest Asset is Proof, Not Just Skills**
Most freshers list tools. You built a complete end-to-end project — data cleaning, modeling, DAX, visualizations, and business insights. That's what separates you. Always lead with the project, not the certifications.

**I Have Breadth That's Rare at Your Level**
SQL + Power BI + DAX + Python + Power Query + Excel covers the full analytics pipeline. Most entry-level candidates are strong in 1–2 tools. Don't undersell this combination.

**Certifications Are Supporting Evidence, Not the Story**
Forage and Analytics Vidhya are credible and recruiters recognize them. But they should back up your project story, not lead it. The project is the headline; certifications are the footnotes.

**I am Targeting the Right Roles Too Narrowly**
"Data Analyst" is competitive. MIS Analyst, Reporting Analyst, BI Analyst, and Operations Analyst roles have lower competition, similar day-to-day work, and are excellent entry points that lead to the same career trajectory.

**Personal Branding is Your Lowest-Hanging Fruit**
A GitHub repo with your Power BI project, a well-optimized LinkedIn headline, and a portfolio link costs you nothing but a few hours — and instantly makes you more discoverable than 90% of candidates at your level.

**GenAI Certifications Are a Timely Differentiator**
My Forage GenAI Data Analytics and Simplilearn Generative AI Studio certs are genuinely relevant in 2025–26 hiring. Mention them actively — many candidates your age don't have them.

**The Gap to Close First: One More Project in a Different Domain**
MY only project is e-commerce. Adding one project in HR analytics, finance, or healthcare data — even on a public dataset — doubles your demonstrated range and removes a common recruiter hesitation about one-trick portfolios.
