Key Learnings: Choosing the Right Claude Model & Effort Level

1. Different Models Have Different Strengths

Haiku → Fastest responses for simple tasks.

Sonnet → Best balance of speed, reasoning, and quality.

Opus → Deepest reasoning for complex research and strategy.


2. Match the Model to the Task

Use lightweight models for simple questions.

Use stronger models for learning, coding, and analysis.

Reserve the most powerful models for high-value work.


3. Effort Level Matters

Low → Quick answers.

Standard → Daily learning and productivity.

High → Complex projects and problem-solving.

Max → Deep research and strategic planning.


4. Context Improves Results

Providing your goal, skill level, and requirements helps Claude generate more accurate and personalized outputs.


5. Don't Overuse Powerful Settings

Using Opus + Max for every task can be inefficient.

The best workflow is choosing the minimum model and effort needed to achieve the desired quality.


6. Build an AI Workflow

A productive workflow is:

1. Learn concepts with Sonnet.

2. Practice skills through projects.

3. Use High effort for project reviews.

4. Use Opus only for major research or career decisions


7. For Data Analytics Students

The most effective setup is:

Primary Model: Claude Sonnet

Default Effort: Standard

High Effort: Projects, portfolio building, interview prep

Max Effort: Deep research and long-term career planning.
