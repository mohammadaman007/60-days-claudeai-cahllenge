What is Prompt Engineering?
Prompt engineering is simply the skill of asking AI tools (like Claude) the RIGHT way to get the answers you want. Think of it like talking to a helpful friend—if you ask a clear, specific question, you'll get a better answer than if you ask something vague or confusing.
A "prompt" is just what you type into Claude. "Prompt engineering" is the practice of writing that prompt in the best possible way.
Why Does It Matter?
Imagine you ask someone: "Make me something delicious." They might make pasta, a salad, or a dessert—you're not sure what you'll get.
But if you ask: "Please make me a chocolate chip cookie with a soft center and crispy edges," they know exactly what to create.
The same is true with AI. The better you describe what you want, the better result you get. Good prompts = good answers. Simple as that.
Three Practical Benefits of Better Prompts

You Get More Accurate Answers — Specific instructions mean Claude understands your actual need instead of guessing.
You Save Time — Instead of asking multiple follow-up questions to fix a mediocre answer, you get what you need in one try.
You Can Use AI for Complex Tasks — Good prompts let you work with Claude on writing, coding, analysis, brainstorming, and more—not just simple Q&A.
