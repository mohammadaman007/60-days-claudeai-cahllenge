**🔑 Key Learnings — Startup Building from Scratch**

**Validation First**
- Never build before validating. Talk to 10 real people first — it's the cheapest research possible.
- A real problem doesn't automatically mean a real business. Frequency + Severity + Willingness to Pay must all be true together.

**Know Your Customer Deeply**
- Generic targeting fails. Define a specific ICP — who, where, what tools, what pain, what budget.
- Build buyer personas with real emotions, quotes, and triggers — not just demographics.

**Market Sizing Matters**
- TAM = total market, SAM = your reachable slice, SOM = what you can realistically win in Year 1.
- A big TAM means nothing if your SOM isn't monetisable.

**MVP = Minimum, Not Maximum**
- Your MVP is the smallest thing that proves someone will pay. For a service business — that's a WhatsApp conversation and a Google Form, not an app.
- MoSCoW framework stops you from overbuilding: Must Have → Should Have → Could Have → Won't Have.

**Pricing Is Always a Hypothesis**
- Start low to get clients. Track your hours. Raise prices after proof. Never finalise pricing before market feedback.

**Competition Teaches You**
- Competitors show you what's working and where the gaps are. Your job is to find the underserved gap — not copy or fear them.

**Risk Is Manageable If Named**
- The biggest risks are predictable: no clients, underpricing, burnout, data privacy, no traction. Name them early and build a mitigation for each.

**Scores & Frameworks Cut Through Emotion**
- Founder-Market Fit Score, Decision Scorecard, MoSCoW, Go/No-Go — frameworks remove bias and give you honest signals.

**Distribution Before Product**
- Knowing how you'll reach customers matters as much as what you're building. LinkedIn + direct outreach beats paid ads every time at zero budget.

**The 30-Day Rule**
- If you don't have a paying client in 30–45 days, something in your model needs fixing. Speed of learning beats perfection of execution.
> **Problem → ICP → Pain Points → MVP → Price → Sell → Systemise → Scale**

Everything else is noise until you complete that sequence in order.
