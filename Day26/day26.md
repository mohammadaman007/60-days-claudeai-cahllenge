Key Learnings from Building a Prior Authorization Workflow Simulator

1. 🏥 Understanding the Prior Authorization (PA) Process
Learned how healthcare services often require approval before treatment, tests, medications, or procedures move forward.


2. 👤➡️🏥➡️🛡️ Multi-Stakeholder Workflow Coordination
The process involves collaboration between:

Patient

Provider (doctor/hospital)

Payer (insurance company)



3. 📄 Documentation Drives Decisions
Accurate clinical records and required documents strongly influence approval outcomes.


4. 🧠 Medical Necessity Is Central
Coverage decisions are based on whether the requested care is considered medically necessary.


5. ⏳ Operational Efficiency Matters
Delays in submission, missing information, and repeated reviews increase time and reduce efficiency.


6. 🔄 Healthcare Workflows Are Iterative
Outcomes are not only approval or denial—cases may move through:

Pend

Denial

Appeal

Peer-to-Peer Review



7. 🎮 Gamification Improves Learning
Interactive drag-and-drop experiences make complex healthcare operations easier to understand.


8. 📊 Metrics Help Measure Performance
Tracking days elapsed and efficiency score demonstrates how process decisions impact outcomes.


9. 💻 Complex Systems Can Be Simulated Frontend-Only
A realistic workflow simulator can be built using only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with state managed in memory.


10. 🚀 Technology Can Improve Healthcare Education
Visual simulations help explain administrative workflows and make healthcare operations more accessible to learners.



Main takeaway:
Healthcare decisions depend not only on treatment needs, but also on coordination, evidence, timing, and process quality.
